# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers of the Capybara Framework pledge to:

- Be respectful, considerate, and inclusive
- Communicate openly and constructively
- Assume good intent and give feedback with care
- Help others when possible and share knowledge

This applies to all communication channels, including GitHub issues, pull requests, emails, forums, and other community spaces related to Capybara.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusion
- Personal attacks or offensive language
- Public or private shaming of contributors
- Ignoring community norms or guidelines

## Enforcement

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behavior deemed unacceptable.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the Capybara Framework community.
